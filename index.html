Gibber

Gibber takes its name from Gibberellins, a group of plant hormones that regulate plant developmental processes.

Each year I miss sowing seeds in time for my garden - either because I forget or because I miss my sowing window. After constantly referencing an old gardening calendar I was inspired to make my own app to solve the issue.

Users on Gibber can search for crops by common or scientific names. Results from the API indicate if the crop should be only grown once per season or may be grown in succession. Users may select crops according to their desired planting method (sown indoors, sown directly, or planted as starts).

Selected crops are included on a secondary list where users may export the planting dates and relevant information to their personal online calendars. Planting dates will also appear on Gibberâ€™s built in calendar.

Yards Biodiversity

Yards Biodiversity allows users to sign up and add plants that are in their immediate vicinity.

The user may select plants from an API I created or add a custom plant of their own. Using information from the USDA, Yards Biodiversity can determine the ratio of native, nonnative, or invasive plants to give a user a better understanding of the flora diversity in their immediate area.


Technologies Used

  • Django Rest Framework

  • Python

  • JavaScript

  • Vue.js
